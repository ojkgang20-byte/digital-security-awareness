If you become a victim of cybercrime:

  • 🏦 Contact your bank immediately to stop fraudulent transactions.
  • 👮 Report the matter to your nearest South African Police Service (SAPS) station.
  • 💳 If your bank card is compromised, block it immediately using your bank's official app or helpline.
  • 📱 Change the passwords for any affected online accounts and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Remember: The sooner you report a cybercrime, the greater the chance of limiting the damage.

Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on your email, banking, social media and other important accounts. Even if someone steals your password, MFA provides an extra layer of security.

DSA Tip: Never approve an authentication request that you did not initiate.

Fake Banking Verification Messages

Cybercriminals are sending fake SMS messages, emails and WhatsApp messages pretending to be banks. They ask victims to click links or provide OTPs, PINs or passwords. Never share your banking PIN, password or One-Time PIN (OTP) with anyone. Banks will never ask for this information through SMS, WhatsApp or email.

Stay Alert. Stay Secure. Verify Before You Click.
