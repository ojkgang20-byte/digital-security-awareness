🚨 Latest Scam Alert

Fake Banking Verification Messages

Cybercriminals are sending fake SMS messages, emails and WhatsApp messages pretending to be banks. They attempt to steal passwords, PINs and One-Time Passwords (OTPs).

Never share your banking PIN, password or OTP with anyone. Always verify suspicious messages through official channels.

DSA Advice: Stop. Think. Verify before you click.

💡 Cyber Safety Tip of the Week

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Protect your email, banking, social media and other important accounts by enabling Multi-Factor Authentication. It provides an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised.

📞 Emergency Reporting Contacts

  • 🏦 Contact your bank immediately if you suspect fraud.
  • 👮 Report cybercrime at your nearest SAPS station.
  • 💳 Block compromised bank cards immediately.
  • 🔒 Change passwords and enable MFA.
